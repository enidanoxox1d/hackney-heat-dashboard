How to Read the Score

This dashboard combines school inputs into one overall Net Heat Resilience Score. It is designed to give users a quick view of which schools may need the most heat-resilience support.

What the main score means Click to expand or collapse
  • 76–100: Strong heat resilience
  • 51–75: Moderate / improving
  • 26–50: High concern
  • 0–25: Very high concern

Higher values mean the school appears more prepared for heat risk. Lower values mean higher concern and greater priority for support.

What the three category scores mean Click to expand or collapse
  • Environment: surrounding heat exposure, tree canopy, green space, and local temperature conditions.
  • Built Environment: building features such as ventilation, shading, roof treatment, water access, and cooling provision.
  • Socio-economic / Preparedness: training, communication, operational planning, and wider school preparedness.

These coloured boxes show how each category is affecting the school's overall score. More negative values mean that category is currently dragging the score down.

How the score is calculated Click to expand or collapse
  1. Each criterion is scored using the index rules. Negative criteria use 0, -0.5, or -1. Positive criteria use 0, 0.5, or 1.
  2. Each criterion score is multiplied by its weight to create a contribution.
  3. Contributions are added within the three categories and then combined into a Final Raw Score.
  4. The raw score is then converted into the Net Heat Resilience Score (0–100) shown on the left.

User guide

How to Use This Dashboard

This page explains the full workflow in a simple way. The dashboard is designed for schools, local partners, or project reviewers to understand how heat-risk data becomes a school priority map.

1

Select a school

Start from Step 1: School Data Input. Choose one of the Hackney schools from the dropdown menu. The form will show the current input values for that school.

2

Score each criterion

Open the Environment, Built Environment, and Socio-economic / Preparedness sections. For each row, choose the score that best matches the school’s situation.

Negative criteria: 0, -0.5, or -1 Positive criteria: 0, 0.5, or 1
3

Read the Net Heat Resilience Score

The dashboard automatically calculates the school’s Net Heat Resilience Score. A higher score means stronger heat resilience. A lower score means higher concern and greater need for support.

4

Save the school profile

Click Save school score after changing the input values. This updates the school’s result, map marker, selected-school profile, and priority ranking.

5

Go to the map dashboard

Open Step 2: Map Dashboard. Click a marker or search for a school name. The side panel will explain the selected school’s score in a more qualitative way.

6

Use the ranking for prioritisation

The ranking table places the schools with the lowest heat resilience scores first. The top three schools are shown immediately, while the remaining schools can be expanded below.

What users should remember

Important notes

  • This is a prototype, so the current values are illustrative demo values unless replaced with real school data.
  • The score is not meant to blame schools. It helps identify where support, investment, or planning may be most needed.
  • The map and ranking are only as accurate as the input data, so data should be reviewed before final decision-making.
